Chop, Dice, Mince

The phrase "chop, dice, mince" comes from three different knife cuts in cooking. Simply, to chop means to cut into big pieces, to dice means to cut into medium pieces, and to mince means to cut into small pieces. Another meaning of "dice" is to cut into dice-shaped pieces (cubes).

The idea of "chop, dice, mince" is similar to other ideas, such as top-down programming (also called "stepwise refinement"), and divide-and-conquer.
